Recent Trends of CFPB Complaints on student loans

By Seann McCombie

The CFPS has received an overall increase in student loan complaints over the last 15 years, accumulating at over 122,000 since they began accepting that. The CFPB began accepting complaints about private student loans in 2012, with complaints about the servicing of federal student loans being added in 2016. The difference in acceptance phases affected the earlier year data. The most significant spikes in complaints coincide with major federal student loan system changes. Some examples of these changes include enforcement actions against major services, the expansion of complaint categories, and the federal student loan payment pause.

There was a consistent downward trend in the number of complaints from 2017 through 2021. There was a 59% increase in total student loan complaints compared to the previous year from 2021-2022, reversing the downward trend. Complaints would begin to increase again in 2023 following the restart of federal student loan payments in October. Servicemembers submitted a higher number of student loan complaints. Many borrowers faced difficulties accessing income-driven repayment plans and experienced other errors. Reports covering 2024 indicate high records of complaints regarding service errors, payment processing problems, and inaccurate information regarding payment plans. The main reasons for complaints are these servicer errors, as well as inaccurate information, customer service issues, and processing delays.
